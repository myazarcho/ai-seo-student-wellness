# Step 9 — Second Article Plan

## Article Title

How to Prioritize Assignments When Everything Feels Urgent

## Primary Keyword

`how to prioritize assignments`

## Secondary Keywords

- assignment priority list
- urgent and important tasks for students
- study task prioritization
- university assignment planning
- multiple assignment deadlines

## Content Pillar

Assignment Management

## Search Intent

The search intent is **problem-solving**.

The user has several tasks and wants a simple method to decide which assignment should be completed first.

## Target Audience

University students who have several assignments, exams, presentations, or research tasks happening within a short period.

## User Problem

The student feels that every task is urgent and may choose work based on:

- The easiest task
- The task they like most
- The closest deadline only
- The task causing the most anxiety

This can lead to poor prioritization.

## Article Goal

The article should help students compare tasks using:

- Deadline
- Importance
- Estimated workload
- Consequences of delay
- Dependency on other people
- Current progress

## Recommended Structure

# How to Prioritize Assignments When Everything Feels Urgent

## Why Everything Can Feel Equally Important

Explain that emotional pressure can make all tasks appear urgent.

## Step 1: List Every Assignment

Create one complete task list.

## Step 2: Write the Deadline

Record the official deadline for every task.

## Step 3: Estimate the Workload

Estimate how much time each task requires.

## Step 4: Check the Importance

Consider:

- Grade value
- Academic impact
- Required presentation
- Group dependency
- Research importance

## Step 5: Identify Task Dependencies

Ask:

- Does another student need my work?
- Do I need professor feedback?
- Do I need library access?
- Does one task need to be completed before another?

## Step 6: Use a Simple Priority System

### Priority 1

Urgent, important, and high-workload tasks.

### Priority 2

Important tasks that require early preparation.

### Priority 3

Small tasks with flexible timing.

## Step 7: Choose One Main Task for Today

Select one major task and one smaller backup task.

## Step 8: Review Priorities Each Day

Priorities may change because of new deadlines, feedback, or unexpected delays.

## Example Priority Table

| Assignment | Deadline | Workload | Importance | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Research report | Friday | High | High | 1 |
| Reading response | Wednesday | Low | Medium | 2 |
| Slide formatting | Thursday | Low | Low | 3 |

## Final Checklist

- Have I listed every task?
- Have I checked the real deadline?
- Have I estimated the workload?
- Have I considered academic importance?
- Does another person depend on my work?
- What is today's main task?
