# Step 4 — User Search Journey

## User Scenario

Maya is an international master's student in Japan. She has three assignments, one presentation, and research work due within the same week.

She feels overwhelmed because she does not know which task to start first. She wants a simple and realistic way to organize her workload.

## Main Search Query

`how to manage multiple assignment deadlines`

## Related Search Queries

- how to prioritize university assignments
- study plan for multiple deadlines
- feeling overwhelmed with university work
- time management for international students
- how to ask a professor for help with workload

## Search Intent

The primary search intent is **informational and problem-solving**.

The student is not only trying to understand academic stress. She wants practical steps that she can use immediately.

## User Search Journey

### Stage 1 — Problem Awareness

The student realizes that several deadlines are approaching and feels confused about where to begin.

### Stage 2 — Search

The student searches Google using a phrase such as:

`how to manage multiple assignment deadlines`

### Stage 3 — Content Discovery

The student sees an article titled:

**How to Manage Multiple Assignment Deadlines Without Feeling Overwhelmed**

The title matches the problem clearly, so the student opens the article.

### Stage 4 — Content Consumption

The student expects the article to provide:

- A simple way to list all assignments
- A method for prioritizing tasks
- Guidance for breaking large tasks into smaller steps
- A realistic weekly study plan
- Advice about when to ask for academic support

### Stage 5 — Action

After reading the article, the student:

1. Writes down every assignment and deadline
2. Estimates how much work each task requires
3. Breaks large assignments into smaller tasks
4. Creates a realistic weekly plan
5. Contacts a professor or academic adviser if the workload is not manageable

### Stage 6 — Desired Outcome

The student should feel more organized and understand the next practical step.

The article should not promise to remove stress completely. It should support realistic planning and encourage appropriate university support when necessary.

## Content Opportunity

The content should be:

- Easy to understand
- Practical
- Non-clinical
- Emotionally supportive
- Search-friendly
- Clear about professional and university support boundaries
