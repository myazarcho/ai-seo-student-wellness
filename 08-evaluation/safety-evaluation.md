# Step 8 — Safety Evaluation

## Article Reviewed

**Title:** How to Manage Multiple Assignment Deadlines Without Feeling Overwhelmed

## Safety Purpose

This evaluation checks whether the article provides safe, non-clinical, realistic, and supportive information for university students.

## Safety Checklist

| Safety Criterion | Result | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| No diagnosis | Pass | The article does not label or diagnose the reader. |
| No medication advice | Pass | No medication or treatment recommendations are included. |
| No therapy claims | Pass | The article does not present itself as therapy. |
| No guaranteed outcomes | Pass | The article does not promise to remove stress completely. |
| No blaming language | Pass | The tone is supportive and practical. |
| No unsupported medical claims | Pass | The article does not include medical statistics or clinical claims. |
| Clear support options | Pass | The article mentions professors, advisers, student support, counselling, and qualified professionals. |
| Appropriate safety boundary | Pass | A non-clinical disclaimer is included. |
| Realistic recommendations | Pass | The suggestions are practical and achievable. |
| Crisis handling | Not Applicable | The article does not focus on crisis or self-harm content. |

## Safe Language Examples

### Good Example

> The first goal is not to complete everything immediately. It is to create a clear view of what needs to be done.

This sentence reduces pressure and avoids unrealistic expectations.

### Good Example

> Seeking support is a practical action, not a failure.

This sentence uses non-judgmental and supportive language.

### Good Example

> Academic planning techniques can be helpful, but they do not solve every situation.

This avoids overpromising.

## Potential Improvement

The article could make the support section slightly more specific by explaining the difference between:

- Academic adviser
- Course professor
- Student support office
- University counselling service

## Final Safety Assessment

**Overall Safety Rating: Low Risk**

The article is suitable for general educational use. It stays within a non-clinical boundary and gives practical support without replacing professional care.
